youtube

It is becoming increasingly clear to me that youtube has to go.

I'm sure you've felt it. Content becoming formulaic, major creators suffering burnout,
harmless videos losing ad revenue or being copyright claimed, forcing creators to leave
or turn to patreon make a living.

But where else can they go? No other platform offers the same caliber of hosting,
built-in ad revenue, and free advertising. It is not just the largest video hosting website,
but one that provides an easy way of having your content found automatically through the algorithm.
A creator would do themselves a disservice posting video on any other platform. In fact, youtube is
so lucrative, it seems to swallow up traditionally non-video genres: movie reviews come to mind.

We can formulate the "Obstacles to Youtube Replacement", then, as follows:

  1. Youtube provides faster and more reliable hosting than feasable for any competitor
  2. Youtube is free
  3. Youtube allows users to profit off videos more than any competitor
  4. Youtube provides free access to viral marketing through the algorithm
  5. Youtube is already popular
  6. Youtube is backed by one of the largest companies in the world
